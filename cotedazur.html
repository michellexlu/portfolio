CÔTE D'AZUR, FRANCE

The Côte d'Azur (or French Riviera) will always hold a special place in my heart as the only place I have ever lived outside of the United States. I spent a semester abroad in this quaint coastal town just on the outskirts of Nice called Antibes. I had been studying French for 10 years prior, and I felt that the best way to achieve true fluency in the language would be to immerse myself directly in a small town of the native-speaking country. During my short-lived time there (thanks to COVID-19), I accomplished my goal of strengthening my conversational French skills while creating lasting friendships and making memories of a lifetime.

Must-Dos for My Favorite Stops in the French Riviera

ANTIBES

After living in this beautiful town for a little over 2 months, it had to be first on my list. Although you can generally expect a peaceful, more relaxing visit to Antibes, there are, nonetheless, endless activities to do and things to see.

If you visit in the summertime, the beaches and narrow cobblestone streets will be swamped with visitors from all over the world, while the ports will be full of luxury yachts. It is located right in between Nice and Cannes, serving as a laid-back vacation getaway from those larger cities.

On the other hand, I had the opportunity to experience Antibes during its winter season, which is much quieter with minimal tourists. The only real downside of visiting during the off-season was that some shops and restaurants had shorter opening hours. However, the serene ambiance and authentic experience of living among the locals made this small inconvenience completely worth it. The weather also remained relatively warm (at least in my Bostonian opinion), staying mostly in the high 50s throughout January-March.

  1. Go on a hike along the Cap d'Antibes
  2. Marvel at the art collection at the Picasso Museum
  3. Try fresh produce and authentic cheeses from the Marché provençal (local farmer's market)
  4. Pick up a sandwich from a local boulangerie (bakery) on your way to La Gravette Beach

    5. My personal go-to was Aux Délices Antibois Fils on Rue de la République. It was right on the way from my apartment to the beach, and they always had the best sandwiches and pastries. Plus, everyone there was always super nice.

  5. Take a photo in front of The Nomad sculpture
  6. Treat yourself to a lavish dinner at Le Figuier de Saint-Esprit (awarded a Michelin star!)

    7. Since we knew our days were numbered in Antibes, my friends and I decided to splurge on one last family dinner. We picked the best Michelin star restaurant in town, and it definitely lived up to our expectations. I ordered escargot as an appetizer and the fish of the day as my entrée– both were delicious but be prepared to break the bank at this restaurant.

  7. Take a swim in the Mediterranean
NICE

I definitely didn't get to spend as much time as I would've liked to in Nice during my time in the French Riviera, but the few short visits I made were enough to make me fall in love with the city.

As the largest city in the region, it's definitely more lively than its charming neighbor Antibes. Nice is famous for its stunning coastline, as well as its alluring art and culture with an abundance of museums, festivals, and beautiful architecture around every corner.

The other thing I love about Nice is how easy it is to get to other stops along the Riviera. From the city, it was only a short bus ride back to Antibes and a quick train ride to Cannes, Monaco, and other coastal towns that I highly recommend visiting if you have the time (like Villefranche-sur-Mer).

  1. Take a stroll along the Promenade des Anglais

    2. This boulevard is probably the most popular attraction in Nice. It overlooks the sea and is lined with luxurious hotels, restaurants, shops, and beaches. At one end is the entrance to Old Nice, where you can find a lovely market that sells fresh produce. The Nice airport and La Musée des Beaux-Arts de Nice (Museum of Fine Arts) lie at the other end.

  2. Discover authentic restaurants and shops in Old Town
  3. Visit the Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art

    4. Be sure to check out the outdoor rooftop on the top floor! It has one of the best views of the city.

  4. See the fun architecture of St. Nicholas Russian Orthodox Cathedral

    5. Although I didn't get to visit the museum myself, I got to see the unique building from afar. The onion domes and vibrant colors of the cathedral make it a top attraction in Nice. Admission is free; however, many visitors suggest reserving a guided tour to explore the inside to its full extent.

  5. Ride the ferris wheel in Place Masséna (main square)
  6. Relax on Beau Rivage beach

    7. Rated one of the best beaches in Nice for its top-notch amenities. It is also the largest in the city. Just a heads up though, the beaches in Nice are different than those you might be used to, since they are all pebbled rather than sandy.

  7. Take a day trip to Eze!

    8. Our program took us on a day trip to Eze, a neighboring village of Nice, and it was one of my favorite excursions of the semester. It is situated on a large hilltop, which allows visitors to experience stunning panoramic views of the sea. We spent most of our time wandering through the cobblestone streets to make our way to the top of the hill. We also toured the famous perfume factory in Eze (Parfumerie Galimard), which was the perfect place to pick up some gifts to bring back home.

MENTON

This small town is nicknamed "the Pearl of France," and once I arrived, I could see why. I immediately fell in love with the rainbow colored buildings perched high atop the hill and stunning views of the Mediterranean. After a day of touring the Italian Riviera, my program stopped here on our way back to Antibes and made it just in time to catch the sunrise, which casted a warm, glowing light over the entire town. It was definitely one of the most beautiful sights I had ever seen.

Menton is smaller and less touristy than Nice and Cannes, offerering a serene alternative with just as much to see and do. It's the last train stop along the Cote d'Azur before hitting the Italian border, making it an easy day trip to make from any of the other larger cities. Menton is known for its abundance of lemon trees, lovely gardens, colorful old town, and pleasant year-round climate.

  1. Visit the Jean Cocteau Museum

    2. Jean Cocteau was a French artist and writer who developed a special connection with Menton after his first experience there. This museum was built in dedication to Cocteau and showcases nearly 1000 works of his art.

  2. Marvel at the Basilica of Saint Michael Archangel

    3. I was especially captivated by the beauty of this basilica, as it sits on top of the hill, overlooking the port and old town. I didn't get a chance to see the inside, but based on the grandeur of the exterior, I could only imagine how opulent the inside must be.

  3. Explore the Val Rahmeh Botanical Garden

    4. This exotic botanical garden houses tons of unique plant species. It'll take you right out of France and into the tropics.

  4. Walk through the Old Town

    5. I spent most of my limited time in Menton wandering down the narrow streets of the town and taking in the charming architecture of the quaint apartments.

  5. Get a whiff of fresh lemons at La Citronneraie

    6. Because of its sub-tropical climate, Menton is the perfect place for growing lemons. This lemon grove is dominated by "Citron de Menton" lemons, which is considered the best type of lemon in the Citrus group.

  6. Attend the la Fête du Citron (Lemon Festival)

    7. If you're lucky and happen to visit at the end of winter, you must attend this famous annual event. This festival attracts hundreds of thousands of tourists, who flock to Menton to see massive sculptures completely made from lemons and oranges.

  7. Catch the sunset at Plage des Sablettes

    8. This beach offers a wonderful view of both the water and the colorful buildings on the hill. It's a great place for watching the sunset, as everything glows under the warm sunlight.

  8. Eat at Mirazur, a 3-Michelin star restaurant

    9. If you're feeling lavish, treat yourself to a fancy dinner at Mirazur, a restaurant that consistently makes the list of world's 50 best restaurants. It serves upscale French cuisine with a little bit of Italian influence, as the town sits so close to the border of Italy. Be sure to plan ahead though (I'm talking like months ahead), since securing a reservation at this high-class restaurant is a challenging feat.

CANNES

Unfortunately, I didn't get to spend much time in Cannes. I mostly went into the city to enjoy its bustling nightlife, but I would've loved to explore more of the area during the day. Cannes is known as a hotspot for the rich and famous, which makes sense from all of the high-end stores and trendy restaurants/bars around the city. I'm sure you've also probably heard of the Cannes Film Festival, which is hosted at the Palais des Festivals et des Congrès.

Since I didn't get to go to these places myself, the list of activities below are recommended must-dos from friends and locals. Hopefully others will be able to enjoy them instead, and I'm determined to eventually make my way back!

  1. Walk along the flashy Boulevard de la Croisette lined with upscale shops and hotels
  2. Attend a movie showing at the Cannes Film Festival (if you're lucky!)
  3. Visit the Notre-Dame de l'Espérance (church)
  4. Watch the yachts and boats dock in Le Vieux Port (Old Port)
  5. Admire the Painted Walls of Cannes
  6. Grab a bite to eat from Marché Forville– another local farmer's market
