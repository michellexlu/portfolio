GREEK ISLANDS

Every travel agency website is bound to display an image of the iconic blue-domed, white-wallled villages of the Greek islands. If a chance to see this spectacular scenery in person isn't enough of a reason to make a trip, then the food and beaches will surely convince you.

I've had the chance to visit Mykonos and Santorini, which are the 2 most popular islands among the hundreds of inhabited islands belonging to Greece. While the two share many physical similarities, they radiate completely different ambiances. Mykonos is famous for its upbeat and upscale scene; whereas, Santorini has a more relaxed and romantic atmosphere. I'm beyond grateful for having had the opportunity to get a taste of both the rowdy and serene sides to the Greek islands.

How to Travel Mykonos & Santorini

MYKONOS

We started off our trip by flying into Athens, spending a few days there (for which you can find the itinerary on a different page), and then taking a high-speed 2 hour ferry to Mykonos Port.

  1. Spend your first day walking down the streets of Mykonos Town

    2. Every turn you take will lead you to a new colorful street full of restaurants, bars, and boutiques. The beauty of Greece is that stopping along the side of any street will make for a picturesque photo-op. I loved posing with the pink and purple flowers that contrasted the white buildings in the background.

  2. Cool off with a refreshing drink somewhere along Little Venice, looking out at the Aegean Sea

    3. Little Venice is a strip of the island named after the Italian city because of its Venetian-inspired buildings and waterfront view. It's a quaint, romantic neighborhood, where you can find lots of bars and restaurants. This area is also one of the best sunset spots in Mykonos, so be sure to reserve a night for dinner and a view!

  3. Right next to Little Venice is where you'll find the famous windmills of Mykonos.

    4. There are 16 windmills in total, but only 7 sit at the top of the hill in Mykonos Town.

  4. Go island hopping from Mykonos to the nearby Cycladic islands.

    5. The central location of Mykonos makes it the perfect starting point for a day of island-hopping to see some of the smaller Greek islands. We ended up staying on one of the islands and renting a beach cabana for the day- so relaxing!

  5. Almost any restaurant in Mykonos Town is guaranteed to be good, but I highly recommend dinner at M-eating

    6. It serves authentic Mediterranean cuisine and is tucked away on a busy side street. We were seated under the vine-covered verandah, and it was the perfect place to dine and people watch at the same time.

SANTORINI
  1. From Mykonos, take a 2-3 hour ferry to Santorini
  2. Navigate through the small streets of the capital city, Fira
  3. While in Fira, visit the Cathedral of St. John the Baptist

    4. This cathedral is the main Catholic church on Santorini with beautiful architecture and peach-colored exteriors. There are also many museums and art galleries in the area that I recommend wandering through to gain a deeper understanding of Greek culture and history.

  4. MUST DO: Watch the sunset in Oia

    5. Sitting at the top of Oia Castle and watching the sunset was a surreal moment for me. The orange and pink skies complimented the whitewashed buildings perfectly, and you even get a view of the windmills in the distance. The crowd starts forming way before the sun sets, so I suggest getting there early to secure a good spot.

  5. Stop by the quirky Atlantis Books bookstore while exploring the main street of Oia

    6. This bookshop is a hidden gem that transports you into a storybook upon entering. It holds a wide selection of first edition books, and the walls are covered in famous book quotes, posters, and murals. You may even find a few cats perched on top of a stack of books in an attempt to escape the heat.

  6. Try authentic Greek tapas

    7. One of the best meals I had during this trip was at Melitini, a quaint Greek tapas restaurant located in Oia. Tapas are a fun way to taste test a bunch of different greek dishes, especially with a large group of people. You really can't go wrong with any of the spreads, seafood, or meats.

  7. If you're feeling adventurous, go parasailing!

    8. As an adventure junkie, I had a blast parasailing over the Aegean Sea. What better view of the island could you get than one from the sky?

What to Know Before Your Visit

  • If you don't plan on renting a car, getting around within the islands by bus is your best and most cost effective bet.

    • And if you do want to drive yourself, you'll have to obtain an International Driving Permit beforehand.

  • Most hotels are located outside of the downtown area (and they're cheaper too!)
  • The islands are big on using cash, so make sure to withdraw plenty before your trip.
  • The summer season (June-August) is by far the best time to visit weather-wise, but that also means those months attract the most tourists.
  • Make sure to bring comfortable shoes! You'll be doing a lot of walking and the streets can be quite steep.
  • The locals are all very much used to tourists, so almost everyone I encountered spoke and understood English.

    • However, it's always nice to learn a few common words and phrases in the native language before arriving in a foreign country. For example, 'efharisto' means 'thank you' in Greek.
