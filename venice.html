VENICE, ITALY

I can wholeheartedly confirm that Venice is just as magical as it looks in all of the movies and photos. It is one of the most beautiful cities I have ever laid eyes on. Tourists mainly visit for the canals that take the place of roads throughout the city, since the entire region is built on water. The blue water of the Adriatic Sea contrasted with the red roofs of the buildings paint a picturesque view that seems to have come straight out of a storybook.

Prepare for a diet consisting mostly of pizza, pasta, and gelato, but don't worry! There are so many attractions to see that all of the walking you'll be doing around the city will work the food right off (plus it tastes so good that it's more than worth it).

What to See and Do in Venice

  1. Take a gondola ride through the Canals

    2. You definitely can't visit Venice without taking an iconic gondola ride through the canals. Yes, it may be cliché, but trust me, this unique way of touring the city is well worth it. A 40-minute gondola ride costs 80 euros and any time over will be another 40 euros/20 minutes. The cost for a ride after sunset will surge up to around 100 euros though.

  2. Enjoy the crowd in St. Mark's Square while grabbing a bite to eat and listening to live music

    3. St. Mark's Square, or Piazza San Marco in Italian, lies in the heart of Venice. It is a large open space– the biggest square in the city actually– surrounded by 3 grand attractions: St. Mark's Basilica, Doge's Palace, and Campanile. The square is a great place to stop for midday drinks in between touring the public buildings or a romantic dinner at night when the live music starts.

  3. Tour Doge's Palace

    4. Doge's Palace served as the residence for all past Doges of Venice. The grand, gold-lined interior of the palace is just as breathtaking as the intricate gothic architecture of the exterior. The chamber of the Great Council is one of the most impressive rooms in the palace, as it is a massive room decorated with paintings of previous Doges.

  4. Walk across the famous Ponte de Rialto over the Grand Canal and stop for a photo op!

    5. The Rialto is the oldest bridge across the canal, crossing over at its narrowest point. The bridge is famous for its architectural design, which is why it's always crowded by tourists. From the top of the bridge, you'll find a remarkable view of Venice.

  5. Take a day trip to the nearby small island of Burano

    6. If you have extra time in Venice, Burano is the perfect destination for a quick day trip. It's known as Italy's Rainbow Island because of all of the vibrantly colored homes, making it a great photo-op location. It takes about half an hour to reach from Venice by boat, but the local food and fun colors make it completely worthwhile.

Eat and Drink Your Way Through Venice!

Venetian food places a large emphasis on seafood, so expect many fish dishes when you're there!
