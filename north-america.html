UNITED STATES

STATES VISITED
(25/50)

Despite all of the amazing places I've been, my home country still remains one of my favorites. The diverse landscape of the U.S., ranging from cities to countrysides, mountaintops to beaches, and deserts to forests, allows for endless adventures and sights to see. Regardless of the time of year, you could find yourself skiing down the slopes of Colorado, tanning on the sandy beaches of Florida, or hiking through any of our beautiful national parks. While the U.S. has plenty of major cities and popular tourist attractions to visit, there are also so many hidden gems throughout the country that offer a completely different yet equally as valuable of an experience. Not only does the U.S. house a wide variety of topographical beauty, but it is also known as a melting pot of different nationalities, cultures, and ethnicities. Everywhere you look, you will find people of unique backgrounds living together and sharing their values, traditions, and (most importantly, in my opinion) cuisines with one another.

MUST-DOs

Visit the Vessel in NYC!

If you're looking for excitement, New York City (also known as "The City that Never Sleeps") is the place for you! It's the most populous city in the U.S., where the streets are constantly bustling with people no matter the hour of day. NYC serves as the heart of art, fashion, food, and commerce, giving everyone something to do. One of my favorite spots in the city is the newly built Vessel located in Hudson Yards. Its unique architecture, composed of a spiral staircase with approximately 2,500 steps, and breathtaking view of the city from the top of the structure attracts visitors from all over the world.

Take a Tropical Vacation to Hawaii!

Hawaii is famous for its colorful beaches, natural beauty, and plethora of outdoor activities. I visited the 3 islands of Big Island, Oahu, and Maui- each with its own unique charm. I would suggest all nature lovers to put Big Island on their bucket list ASAP, as it was hard to believe that the incredible volcanos, rainforests, and cliffs before my eyes were truly real life. In a quick 180, Oahu radiated a lively city atmosphere with crowded beaches, luxury shopping, and rowdy nightlife. Honolulu, the capital city, is located on Oahu; however, that doesn't mean that you won't still be surrounded by plenty of natural attractions. Lastly, Maui lies in the middleground of the other two. It offers a calmer vibe than Oahu yet the small beach towns of Maui provide better insight into the people and local cultures than does Big Island.

Spend a Summer in Martha's Vineyard!

If you find yourself on the East Coast, you won't regret paying a visit to the beautifiul Island of Martha's Vineyard. There's a reason why many of your favorite celebrities flock here for the summer, including the Obamas, Clintons, and Kennedys among many others. The island is comprised of 6 towns: Edgartown, Oak Bluffs, Vineyard Haven, West Tisbury, Aquinnah, and Chilmark. The photo of the lighthouse above was taken during my high school senior trip in 2017, where we spent a weekend enjoying lobster rolls in Oak Bluffs, watching sunsets on Menemsha Beach in Chilmark, and catching the sunrise on the beaches of Edgartown. Take a Weekend Trip to Downtown Boston!

Of course, it's only appropriate for me to boast about my hometown as a must-see. Walking down the cobblestone streets of Boston will instantly transport you back in history, which earns it the reputation as one of America's most historic cities. I grew up in a suburb outside of Boston, and my favorite weekend activity would be getting on the MBTA train to spend a day in the city. My summer days consisted of shopping on Newbury Street, kayaking in the Charles River, and picniking while people-watching in Boston Common. In the wintertime, I have many fond memories of my parents taking me to watch the planetarium show at the Museum of Science or ice skate at the Boston Common Frog Pond, and we would always end our day with delicious food from Faneuil Hall Marketplace. This city has fun activities to offer all visitors both young and old.
